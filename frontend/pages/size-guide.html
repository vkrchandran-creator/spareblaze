Measuring brake pads: Brake pads are specified as Height × Width × Total Thickness (all in mm). Always replace brake pads in axle pairs (both front-left and front-right, or both rear-left and rear-right) for balanced braking.

1
Check OEM Number

Look for the OEM part number stamped on the backing plate of the old pad.

2
Measure with Calliper

Use a vernier calliper to measure Height, Width, and total Thickness in mm.

3
Match Vehicle

Cross-reference against your vehicle make, model, year, and variant to confirm fitment.

4
Note Friction Grade

EE/FF = standard; GG/HH = high-performance. Match grade to your driving style.

Minimum Safe Thickness: Replace brake pads immediately when the friction material thickness measures less than 3mm. Driving below this threshold significantly reduces stopping power and may damage the brake disc.

Installation Tip: Before fitting the new filter, apply a thin film of clean engine oil to the rubber gasket. Hand-tighten the filter until the gasket makes contact, then turn an additional ¾ turn — do not overtighten with tools as this can damage the sealing surface.

Wiper blade sizes: Blade lengths are specified in millimetres. The driver's side is almost always longer than the passenger side. Most modern Indian cars use the J-hook (also called Type-J or 9×3mm hook) connector. Replace wiper blades every 12 months or when they streak or chatter.

Monsoon Tip: Switch to flat/frameless (beam-type) wiper blades before the rainy season. They maintain uniform pressure across the entire blade length for superior wipe quality and do not clog with dirt and moisture like traditional framed wipers.

Belt identification: Serpentine (Poly-V) belts are identified by a rib code — e.g. 6PK1750 means 6 ribs, 1750mm effective length. Timing belts are identified by tooth count and pitch. Always check your owner's manual for the correct replacement interval.

How to Measure a Brake Pad — Step by Step

1
Remove the wheel and locate the brake calliper

Safely jack the vehicle, remove the wheel, and locate the brake calliper assembly. The brake pad is visible through the calliper window. Do not compress the brake pedal while the wheel is off.

2
Measure total thickness (including backing plate)

Using a vernier calliper, measure the full thickness of the pad — that is, the total dimension from the outer face of the friction material to the back of the metal backing plate. Note this value in mm.

3
Measure height and width of the backing plate

Measure the height (top to bottom) and the width (side to side) of the metal backing plate only — not the friction material. These dimensions are used for fitment matching.

4
Record OEM number and check remaining friction material

Look for the OEM part number stamped or moulded on the backing plate. Also estimate the remaining friction material thickness — if less than 3mm, the pad must be replaced immediately.
